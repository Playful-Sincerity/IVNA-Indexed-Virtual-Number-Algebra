## Our Analysis

### Why subscripts matter
Without subscripts, 0/0 = ???, ∞-∞ = ???, and you can't recover values after dividing by zero. The subscripts carry the information through these operations. With subscripts: 0₃/0₂ = 3/2, ∞₅-∞₃ = ∞₂, and 5/0₁ = ∞₅ → ∞₅·0₁ = 5.

### Shared epsilon — deeper than expected
Kiel's sharpest point. We analyzed the multivariate limit xy/(x²+y²) using IVNA (x=0_a, y=0_b) and found IVNA correctly produces the directional limit ab/(a²+b²) for each direction b/a. Verified with Python (ivna.py), SymPy, and Wolfram — all match classical results.

IVNA handles independent approaches through:
- Different index ratios → different directions
- Different orders (0² vs 0¹) → different rates of approach

The "gap" is narrower than initially thought — only exotic cases where the ratio between infinitesimals is itself non-standard in a way not reducible to orders.
